MODES OF SEA FREIGHTING

Sole Use Container

Shipping container will be for your exclusive use. Depending on the access we are able to position, load and unload containers directly at your residence (except Australia where container must be unloaded into a bonded warehouse when AQIS Inspection is required).

LCL - Less than Container Load

Your goods will be consolidated into a shipping container together with other people's goods. This is generally the most economical method of shipping, as you will only pay for the share of the container your goods take up. With this service we may not be able to confirm specific shipping details at the time we uplift your goods, however we can advise an approximate transit time.

LCL - Less than Container Load

Once your goods have been packed they are placed into a wooden crate(s) or pallet(s). These crate(s) or pallet(s) are then loaded into a shipping container together with other commercial goods. This service is used when you do not have enough goods to have sole use container and there is no groupage container service. On this basis we can provide you with specific shipping details and in some instances provide you with different options.

MODES OF AIR FREIGHTING

Loose

Individual packaged items are sent via air cargo consolidation network. Services can include delivery to 'airport only', or delivery through to your residence. With 'airport only' consignments, freight is prepaid, but all destination services & costs are the responsibility of the owner - notably terminal fees, Customs clearance charges, and any local delivery charges are not prepaid.

Air van

This is much like LCL, but dispatched via air. Goods are consolidated either onto air pallets, or into solid corrugated board outer boxes - 'Air van' / 'Covpacks'. This is done in order to secure smaller packaged items for ease of movement through the air cargo consolidation network.
